Help determine the MVP for Transportation Metropolitan Planning Area Boundary!

Per federal code 23 USC § 134, the metropolitan planning area (MPA) must include at least the existing urbanized area (UZA) as defined by the U.S. Census Bureau and the contiguous area expected to become urbanized within the next 20 years. The Draft MPA Boundary displayed on the map meets these minimum criteria.

Use this Comment Map to identify areas where you think the Draft MPA Boundary should be expanded to account for:
  • Future residential growth (e.g., neighborhoods or areas where new residential development is expected)
  • Existing or future traffic generating destinations (e.g., business and commercial areas, recreational facilities and parks, community facilities such as schools and hospitals)
  • Improved continuity and connectivity of the transportation network and modes (i.e., public transit, driving, walking, biking)
Things to Consider:
  • The MPA boundary should be comprehensive enough to foster an effective planning process that ensures connectivity between different transportation modes, reduces access disadvantages, and promotes efficient transportation investment strategies.
  • You can see the locations and content of other people’s comments on the map. All comments will be compiled and shared publicly at the conclusion of this process. Names and contact info will be kept confidential.
  • The Comment Map is open for your feedback through April 28, 2023.

Thank you for your time and participation in the MPA boundary development process!

Visit the MVP for Transportation website Boundary Development page and FAQ to learn more.
